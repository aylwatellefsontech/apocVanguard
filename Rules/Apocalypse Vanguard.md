# Apocalypse Vanguard

V0.6.2 \- 2026 06 24

Apocalypse Vanguard is a cleaned up fan version of Apocalypse, meant to play games of Apocalypse in a smaller footprint, and faster.  

# Setup Changes

To play vanguard, play with between 100 to 150 points of units, on a 48” x 48”, 44” x 60” or 48” x 60” table.

# Rules Modifications {#rules-modifications}

The following are a rough list of modifications of the original Apocalypse:  
\- When a unit loses ½ its life, reduce the number of models in its unit by ½, rounded up.

## Tactic Deck Construction

Each player creates a deck of 16 command cards \- 8 of those cards should be the ‘base card’ set, the first 8 cards.  The other 8 can come from the extra cards, psychics cards, or from that particular faction’s special cards.

## Relaxed Construction

For Force organisation, use the following changes to the chart organisation chart.  Any requirement of 3-6 or 3-5 of a unit is instead 2-6 or 2-5 respectively.

## Supersonic

Reduce the Power of Supersonic units by 2 if they cannot lose Supersonic. During deployment, this unit can be set up in Tactical Reserves. When this unit arrives as reinforcements, set it up anywhere on the field more than 9” from enemy units. At the start of the Reinforcements Step or the End of the Game, if this unit is on the field, put it into Tactical Reserves.  While in Tactical Reserves, it may attack other Supersonic units in Tactical Reserves as if it were in Range. In addition, Subtract 1 from hit rolls for attacks with ranged weapons that target this unit. Melee weapon attacks against this unit miss unless the attacker has Fly.

## Reinforcements

You can, if you wish, play with reinforcements. Starting on the 3rd turn, at the start of each turn, you get 10 reinforcement points, with which you can reinforce units with.  Any points you do not spend can be banked for future turns.

Any units that reinforce are placed within 12” of your table edge, and 9” away from enemy units.  They join the closest Force.  Units should come on at ½ wounds, rounded up. If they have only 1 wound, they should come on with a blast marker. HQ, Lords of War, and Flyers cannot reinforce.

# How to Play {#how-to-play}

To play, you will need to build an army, made up of units of models, lots of dice (d6 and d12s), rulers, command cards, tokens, a board (48”x48” to 48”x72”), and terrain on that board.

To start, you must decide on a points level for your game, and build an appropriately sized army. The normal game sizes are:  
100 Points \- standard game  
125 Points \- medium game  
150 \- larger game

# Army Composition {#army-composition}

Armies are made up of units.

## Units

A unit is a certain number of models of a specific type \- usually, 1, 3, 5, 10 or 20 models.

Units are chosen from faction lists.  
You can use the army builder, or find the list of units here:  
  Each unit has a unit sheet with the following:

**Name** \- the name of the unit.

**Profile** \- each unit may have multiple profiles, with different stats.  
**M** \- The Movement this unit moves in inches  
**WS** \- How good this unit attacks in Melee  
**BS** \- How good this unit attacks at ranged  
**A** \- How many attacks this unit generally has  
**W** \- How much damage this unit can take  
**Ld** \- The likelihood this unit breaks  
**Sv** \- How good this unit is at deflecting damage  
**N** \- How many models this unit is composed of  
**Pt** \- The number of Points this profile is to take.  
**Abilities** \- any special abilities the unit has

**Weapons**  
Each profile will have a number of weapons with the following stats  
**Name** \- the name of the weapon  
**Type** \- the type of weapon, often with special rules attached  
**Rng** \- the Range the weapon can be used at  
**A** \- the number of attacks the unit makes when attacking with the weapon  
**S/AP** \- The effectiveness of the weapon against Infantry vs Armour  
**Abilities** \- any special abilities the weapon has

**Options**  
Units may have options, some of which cost points, or require a specific profile to be chosen. These must be selected at army build time.

### Units of multiple models

Units of 1 model are placed and move individually.

Units of 1-3 models are placed individually, but must always be within ½” of each other at the start and end of each activation.

Units of 5+ models are instead placed as blocks of 5, and each block must be within ½” of another block.

### Blocks.

Use base blocks that have each of the 5 miniatures in the block within ½” of at least 2 other models.  Base blocks act as a single base with 5 models on them. If you do not have a block, you can simulate this by choosing 1 unit as leader of the block, and having the 4 other units within ½” of that leader.

## Army Detachments

Each unit in your army must all have the the same trait of one of the following:  
Imperium, Chaos, Orks, Aeldari, T’au Empire, Tyranids

For 100-125 points, use 3-4 detachments.  For 125-150, use 4-5 detachments. For 150-250, use 5-6 detachments.

Divide up units into detachments, which have a specific number of min and max number units of specific types (see Core Detachments).

Each Detachment must include the units listed for that detachment type. Each unit is of one of the specific role types: HQ, Troops, Elites, Fast Attack, Heavy Support, Transports, Flyers, Fortifications, Lords of War.

Units also have a number of keywords.  All units will have either the Light, Heavy, or Super-Heavy keywords. They may have further keywords such as infantry, vehicle, titanic, flyer, or monster.  
Units also have Faction keywords.

One unit per detachment is then chosen as the commander. If it is a character, it is also a Warlord. You must choose at least 1 warlord in your army.

Then, chose one Warlord to be the Warmaster. Warmasters get 1 Warmaster trait.

If all the units in a detachment are all part of 1 faction, they gain their faction trait.

# Setup {#setup}

Setup the table as per the mission. The size should be roughly based on the size.  
100 to 150 points of units: 48” x 48”, 44” x 60” or 48” x 60” table.  
150 to 250 points: 44” x 60” or 48” x 60”, 48” x 84” table.

Lay down Terrain. In general, there should be at least 1 terrain feature per 24” x 24” section of the board.

## Start Playing

The game is divided into 4 turns.

Players roll off, and add their army’s strategy rating.  The higher roll can choose to either choose deployment and deploy second, or choose who starts with initiative.  The other player gets the option not chosen.

The Players then alternate, deploying detachments.

When deploying a detachment, deploy the HQ as the detachment commander and warlord.  Other units in that detachment must be deployed within 12” of the HQ (at least one model in the unit must be within 12”).  If there are multiple HQ, choose 1 to to act as the commander. If there are no HQ, choose over unit to be the commander.

### Reinforcements

Up to ⅓ your units (unless noted in the mission) can be held back during deployment as Tactical Reserves. Reinforcements not deployed by the end of the game are considered destroyed.

Start the game with 3 chosen command cards.  Shuffle the remainder of your cards into your command deck.  

# Starting the game

A game is played in the phases:  
**Order Phase.**  
**Action Phase.**  
**Damage Phase.**

# Order Phase {#order-phase}

Starting on the second turn, alternate who has initiative.

Each player draws 3 cards, up to 7\. For every Warlord you have, you can draw an additional card, but after drawing those extra cards you must put an equal number back on the top of your deck in any order.

Any reinforcements from Tactical Reserves can be deployed, starting with the player with initiative. If they have no special rules dictating placement, they must be deployed within 6” of your deployment zone table edge within your deployment zone, and further than 9” from enemies.

Units out of command (further than 12” from a commander) count as out of command.

Each player chooses a command for each of their divisions.

There are 3 commands: Assault, Advance, and Aim.

# Action Phase {#action-phase}

When a detachment activates, they perform actions based on the command.  
The commander and up to 6 friendly units haven’t activated yet activate as the detachment. These units must be within 12” (or 24” with infiltrator) or units out of command, and be closer to this commander than any other friendly commander.

  You can choose the order in which they move.

**Assault** \- Your units in that division move, doubling their move value.  They may then fight.

**Advance** \- Your units in that division can move.  Then, they may either shoot or fight.

**Aim** \- Your units can either shoot or fight. Add 1 to shoot to-hit rolls, and subtract 1 from fight to-hit rolls.

## Move {#move}

Move \- Your unit moves that distance in inches. Vertical movement over terrain features counts as part of this movement. Units of size 1-3 move individually, but must stay within ½” of each other. Units of greater size move in blocks of 5, and each block must be within ½” of another block.

### Blocks.

 Use base blocks that have each of the 5 miniatures in the block within ½” of at least 2 other models. If you do not have a block, you can simulate this by choosing 1 unit as leader of the block, and having the 4 other units within ½” of that leader.

### Flight {#flight}

Units with flight can ignore models and vertical terrain when moving.

### Terrain {#terrain}

#### Difficult Terrain {#difficult-terrain}

If any part of the unit moves through difficult terrain, subtract 1” from their movement.

#### Defensible Terrain & Garrisoning {#defensible-terrain-&-garrisoning}

Terrain that can be moved into and provides some form of protection (i.e. a building or ruins) is Defensible Terrain.  
If all your models in a unit are within 6” of a building or defensible terrain, and at least one is within ½”, you can garrison the terrain. Move the full unit within the defensible terrain.

This gives your units the benefits of that terrain, which is usually defensible terrain.  Enemy units cannot garrison already garrisoned terrain. They can fight the unit in garrisoning the terrain if they are within ½” of the defensible terrain, but do not count as engaging that unit.

A garrisoning unit can use their move to leave the terrain, by using their Move action to move within 6” of the defensible terrain.

If the garrison terrain is larger than 6” by 12”, split it up into parts, roughly equal in size and each as close to 6” x 12” or 6” x 6” as possible.

#### Obstacles Terrain {#obstacles-terrain}

Obstacles are terrain that blocks some line of sight, but that units do move into, such as Barricades.  Moving over them requires vertical movement. Units that make shoot attacks where the Line of Sight between the units crosses the obstacle, and the target is within ½” of the obstacle, the target counts as an obscured target.

Units within ½” of the obstacle can attack targets within ½” of the obstacle and within 2” of them with a fight attack, even if they are not engaged.

If you end within ½” of an enemy unit, you are engaging that enemy. Engaged enemies cannot normally shoot, only fight.

### Fall Back {#fall-back}

If your units are engaged with an enemy, you can Fall Back. If you do so, you must end more than 1” from enemy units, and cannot shoot or fight unless you have Titanic or Flight.

#### Out of command

If a unit out of command ends a move within a commander, they lose their out of command token.

### Transports {#transports}

Units that end a move wholly within 6”, with at least one model within ½” of transport can move into it, as long as the Transport can fit the number of models in the unit. They are removed from the table, and are in the transport.

Units can leave a transport if the transport hasn’t moved this turn, moving completely within 6” of the transport onto the battlefield.

For Out of Command for transported units measure from the transport.

If a Transport is destroyed with models inside, place the unit inside within 6” onto the table. Roll a d6 for each unit. On a roll of 1, put a blast marker next to that unit.

## Shoot {#shoot}

Units shoot all their ranged weapons, and can choose different targets for their weapons.  To shoot, roll a number of (A) for the weapon. Each that matches their BS number (e.g. a BS of 3+ requires a 3, 4, 5, or 6\) is a success.  For any that are hit, check the target type. If it is light, make a roll for SAP, otherwise use the SAT roll.  Each roll of those that succeeds causes a blast marker on the hit unit.

Obscured Character \- If you shoot a character that is not the closest enemy, it is obscured.

### Obscured Target {#obscured-target}

Characters targeted when not the closest enemy are obscured targets, as are units obscured by terrain through obstacles or garrisoning terrain. Attacking obscured targets subtract 1 from to-hit rolls against them. Light Characters instead subtract an additional 1 (so \-2) from to-hit rolls against them.

## Fight {#fight}

Units attack with their Melee weapons, and can choose different targets for their weapons. Melee attacks can happen only against enemies engaged (within 1/2” of the unit).  To fight, roll a number of (A) for the weapon. Each that matches their WS number is a success.  For any that are hit, check the target type. If it is Light, make a roll for SAP; otherwise, use the SAT roll.  Each roll of those that succeeds causes a blast marker on the hit unit. Every 2 blast markers turns into a large blast marker.

Units within ½ and inch of the obstacle can attack targets within ½ of the obstacle and within 2” of them with a fight attack, even if they are not engaged.

## Damage Phase {#damage-phase}

At the end of the turn, is the Damage phase.

First, any units that are out of command take damage equal to ½ their starting wounds (they are routed).

Players alternate resolving units with blast markers.

A unit rolls D12s for small blast markers, and D6s for large blast markers. Each that is equal or more than their save is saved. 

Units with blast markers then make a Morale test. They then roll a D6 and add the blast markers on them. If the result is equal or less than their leadership, or the roll is a 1, the test is passed. Otherwise, they take 1 damage.

### Cover {#cover}

Units in defensible terrain gain Cover \- They get a 6+ save instead of their normal save if they desire.

### Failed Saves

Those blasts that are not saved, result in 1 damage on the unit.  Units that have damage up to half their wounds remove ½ the models of the unit (rounded down), and become critically damaged.  They suffer \-1 to hit, and if they are over 1 model. Units that have damage equal or more to their health instead die, and are removed from the table.

In addition, a unit that took 1+ blast markers must make a leadership roll, which is a d12, to reach their leadership. If they fail, they take 1 more damage.  

If a commander is destroyed, nominate a new unit within 12” to be the commander (that is not already a commander). You must prioritize Characters first. If there are no characters, then you can select other units. If there are no units within 12”, choose the closest friendly unit to the destroyed commander.

Super-Heavy destruction. When a Super heavy is destroyed, roll a D12. On a 10+ (it explodes), disembark any unit inside them, then put a blast marker next to each unit within 6” of it.

# Common Abilities {#common-abilities}

The following are common abilities

## Weapon Abilities {#weapon-abilities}

#### Anti-air

Add 2 to hit rolls for attacks made with anti air weapons against units with ‘aircraft’.

#### Barrage

Attacks made with this weapon can target units that are not visible to the Firing unit

#### Destroyer

If a wound roll for an attack made with this weapon is successful, place 2 blast markers next to the target unit instead of one.

#### Inferno

Attacks made with this weapon automatically score a hit (no hit roll is made)

#### One use only

This weapon can make only 1 attack each battle.

#### Rapid Fire

Double the attacks characteristic with this weapon when targeting enemies within ½ the weapon’s maximum range.

#### Sniper

Add 2 to hit rolls for attacks with this weapon against characters.

#### Supercharge

Before a unit attacks with this weapon, they can supercharge it. Until the end of the turn, they add 1 to wound rolls for attacks with this weapon, but every hit roll of 1 causes this unit to take a blast marker.

#### Witch fire 

Reroll wound rolls for attacks with this weapon against targets whose leadership is less then this unit. 

## Unit Abilities {#unit-abilities}

#### Deep Strike

During deployment, this unit can be set up in Tactical Reserves instead of being set up on the battlefield. When this unit arrives as reinforcements, set it up anywhere on the battlefield that is more than 9” from any enemy units. No more than ½ the total number of units in your army can be set up in Tactical Reserves, even if every unit in your army has this ability.

#### Ignore Damage (+X) 

Each time you fail a saving throw for this unit and would take a damage, roll a d6. If the roll equals or exceeds the number in brackets, the unit does not gain the damage marker. If can only use 1 Ignore Damage ability per damage.

#### Infiltrators

When this unit is set up on the battlefield, it can be set up anywhere that is wholly within 24” of its Detachment Commander, and outside of the enemy player’s deployment, and further than 9” enemy units.  They are not Out of Command unless they are further than 24” of their Detachment Commander. Their cover save for garrisoning defensible terrain is also increased to a 5+.

#### Objective Secured

If 1 or units with this ability are within 6” of an objective marker, and no enemy units have this ability, they control that objective marker.

#### Open-topped

Units embarked in this unit can make shoot actions, measuring distance and line of sight from the transport. The embarked unit suffers restriction and negative modifiers applied to the transport.

#### Stealth

Subtract 1 from hit rolls for attacks made with ranged weapons that target this unit.

#### Super-Heavy

Super Heavy that carry out Aimed Fire or Advance can make one shoot action and one fight action (instead of only 1 of these).

#### Supersonic (old, deprecated)

Subtract 1 from hit roll for attacks made with ranged weapons that target this unit. Fight attacks automatically miss unless the attacker has Fly. (Note \- the army list Power should increase by 2 if the unit cannot lose supersonic).

#### Supersonic (New)

During deployment, this unit can be set up in Tactical Reserves. When this unit arrives as reinforcements, set it up anywhere on the field more than 9” from enemy units. At the start of the Reinforcements Step or the End of the Game, if this unit is on the field, put it into Tactical Reserves.  While in Tactical Reserves, it may attack other Supersonic units in Tactical Reserves as if it were in Range. In addition, Subtract 1 from hit rolls for attacks with ranged weapons that target this unit. Melee weapon attacks against this unit miss unless the attacker has Fly.

#### Harrier (New)

At the start of the round, can gain or lose Supersonic.

#### Terror Troops

Subtract 1 from the leadership characteristics of units within 6" of enemy units with this ability.

# Warmaster Traits {#warmaster-traits}

You can either roll or choose a Warmaster trait

1. #### Master Strategist

While this unit is alive, you have a max strategic hand of 8, and start the battle with 1 additional asset.

2. #### Righteous Fury

Add 1 to this unit’s attack characteristic.

3. #### Stealth Adept

This unit can only be chosen as the target of an attack made with a ranged weapon if it is the closest enemy unit to the firing unit.

4. #### Master-crafted Weapons

Add 1 to wound rolls for this unit’s attacks.

5. #### Supreme Tactician

Roll one D6 if this unit is on the battlefield at the start of the Orders phase, or is embarked on a Transport that is on the battlefield at the start of the Orders phase. On a 4+, you can generate on extra command asset.

6. #### Heroic Commander

Increase this unit’s aura abilities that affect friendly units by 6”. If they have no aura abilities, instead friendly units within 6” add 1 to their Leadership characteristic.

7. #### Inspiring Presence

Add 1 to the Leadership characteristic of friendly units whilst they are within 12” of this unit.

8. #### Fearsome Reputation

Enemy units subtract 1 from their Leadership characteristic whilst they are within 6” of this unit.

9. #### Martial Discipline

Friendly units that are within 6” of this unit when they Fall Back can still make Shoot and or Fight actions this phase.

10. #### Head-taker

You can re-roll wounds for attacks made by this unit that target enemy characters.

11. #### Born Survivor

Add 1 to this unit’s wounds characteristic.

12. #### Relic Force Field

Once per Damage phase, you can roll a D12 for one of this unit’s saving throws instead of a D6.

# Apocalypse Missions {#apocalypse-missions}

The first mission is Apocalyptic Assault. For subsequent missions, use the Winds of War Mission Generator.

## Mission: Apocalyptic Assault {#mission:-apocalyptic-assault}

Armies. Armies are built as normal. The player with the highest power level is the Attacker.

#### Setup

Set up the field as normal. If you are using Warzones, choose the Warzone.

Alternate placing 6 objective markers, starting with the Attacker. Objective markers cannot be placed within 12” of any table edge or each other.

#### Deployment.

 Each player’s deployment zone is a rectangle starting on the long table edge, and ending 12” from the centre of the table.  The Defender chooses which deployment zone is theirs.

#### Slay the Warlord

At the end of the turn, players draw a Command asset for each enemy Warlord they destroyed, and additional one if it was the Warmaster.

#### Battle Length

The game ends if 1 player has no units, or the end of the 5th turn.

#### Victory Conditions

At the end of each turn, the player who controls more objective markers scores 1 victory point.  If they tie, the Defender gets 1 victory point. If the Attacker’s power level is 25% higher, the Defender gets 2 victory points instead.

The player with the most victory points is the winner.

# Winds of War {#winds-of-war}

Winds of War is the primary way to generate missions if you are not playing a player-made mission (the other normal way to play).

## Mission Generator

Set up the table.  If you are using Warzones, pick the appropriate Warzone.

The player whose army has more points is the Attacker. They start with initiative.

A game is played until only 1 player has units on the table, or 5 turns have passed.

The player that wins is the one with the most Victory points.

Roll on the following tables in order: Deployment, Objective, Twist, Ruse, and Sudden Death generator tables.

## Deployment Table {#deployment-table}

Attacker rolls one D6 and uses the appropriate Deployment zone. the Defender then chooses which side is their deployment zone.

1. #### Spearhead Assault

Each player’s edge is the short table side. Their Deployment is a pentagon where the point is 12” from the centre, and goes to 24” from their table edge on the long edge table.

2. #### Dawn of War

Each player’s edge is the the wide side of the table. Their zones are rectangles, 12” from the centre.

3. #### Search and Destroy

Divide the tables into quarters, and players deploy on opposite sides.  Do not deploy 18” from the centre.

4. #### Hammer and Anvil

Players deploy on short sides of the table.  Their deployment zones are rectangles, 18” from the centre table.

5. #### Front-Line Assault

Players deploy on the long sides of the table. Their Deployment is a pentagon where the point is 12” from the centre, and goes to 12” from their table edge on the short edge table.

6. #### Vanguard Strike

Draw a line between 2 opposite corners. Players each have different sides, but cannot deploy within 12” of the line.

## Objective Table {#objective-table}

The Attacker rolls a D12 and uses the appropriate Objective. This is how players win.

1. #### Field of Glory

Place one objective marker at the centre of the battlefield. Starting with the Attacker, each player sets up on objective marker in their deployment zone, more than 12” from any battlefield edge. At the end of each turn, each player scores 1 victory point for each objective marker that they control.

2. #### Invasion

Starting with the Attacker, each player places one objective marker in their deployment zone, more than 12” from any battlefield edge. At the end of each turn, a player scores 1 victory point if they control both objective markers

3. #### War of Attrition

If more enemy units were destroyed during this turn than friendly units, you score 1 victory point at the end of the turn.

4. #### The Prize

Place one objective marker at the centre of the battlefield. A unit can pick it up by ending a move on top of it. The objective marker moves with that unit from then on. The unit can drop the objective marker at any time, and most drop it if they are destroyed or if that unit leaves the battlefield for any reason. If it is dropped by a player’s unit, their opponent places the objective marker within 1” of the unit that dropped it. If a unit from one player’s army is carrying the objective marker at the end of the battle, they score 5 victory points.

5. #### Drawn and Quartered

Starting with the Attack, the players alternate placing objective markers on the battlefield until four have been placed. Objective markers cannot be set up within 18” of any battlefield edge, or within 18” of each other. At the end of each turn, the player who controls the most objective markers scores 1 victory point (if the players control the same number, they each score 1 victory point).

6. #### Burn and Pillage

Starting with the Attacker, the players alternate placing objective markers on the battlefield until six have been placed. Objective markers must be set up within a player’s own deployment zone, and cannot be set up within 12” of any battlefield edge, or within 12” of each other. At the end of each turn, a player who controls an objective marker in the enemy deployment zone can destroy it \- remove the objective marker when doing so. Each player score 2 victory points for each objective marker in the enemy’s deployment zone that has been destroyed.

7. #### Show of Force

Each player scores one victor point each time an enemy Detachment is completely destroyed during the battle. If the enemy Detachment was a Super-heavy Detachment, score 3 victory points instead.

8. #### Take and Hold

Place one objective marker at the centre of the battlefield. Starting with the Attacker, the players then alternate placing additional objective markers on the battlefield until five have been placed (including the one in the centre). Objective markers cannot be places within 12” of any battlefield edge, or within 18” of each other. At the end of each turn, the player who controls the most objective markers scores 1 victory point (if the players control the same number, they each score 1 victory point) and the player who controls the objective marker in the centre of the battlefield scores 1 additional victory point.

9. #### Glory Seeker

Each player scores 1 victory point each time an enemy unit with a Power Rating of 10 or more is destroyed. In addition, each player scores 1 additional victory point each time an enemy Super-Heavy unit is destroyed.

10. #### Starfall

Starting with the Attacker, the players alternate placing objective markers on the battlefield until six have been placed. Each must be individually numbered, 1 through 6\. Objective markers cannot be set up within 18” of ny battlefield edge, or within 18” of each other. At the start of the third turn, the Attacker rolls a D6 to determine the landing site of the comet \- this cannot be re-rolled for any reason. Remove all objective markers from the battlefield other than the one corresponding to the number rolled; whichever player controls the remaining objective marker at the end of the battle scores 3 victory points.

11. #### Kingslayer

Each player scores 1 victory point each time an enemy Warlord is destroyed, and 1 additional victory point if the enemy Warmaster is destroyed.

12. #### Supply Cache

Starting with the Attacker, the players alternate placing objective markers on the battlefield until six have been placed. Each must be individually numbered, 1 through 6\. Objective markers cannot be placed within 18” of any battlefield edge, or within 18” of each other. At the end of each turn, the players alternate selecting an objective marker they control and rolling one D12 for it, beginning with the player who had the Initiative in that turn. On a 10+, they have found the cache and all other objective markers are removed from the battlefield; whichever player controls the remaining objective marker at the end of the battle scores 3 victory points (if the cache has not been found by the end of the battle, then no player scores any victory points).

## 

## Twist Table {#twist-table}

The Attacker rolls a D12 to choose the appropriate Twist.

1. #### Dead of Night

During the first turn, the maximum range of all ranged weapons is limited to 24”. Targets that are further away are not visible. During the second turn, the max range of all weapons is limited to 36”; During the third turn the max range is 48”, and so on.

2. #### Witch Hunter

When players generate Command Assets in the Generate Command Assets step, each can roll on D6 for each of their Detachments that include one or more Psykers; on a 4+ they can generate one additional Command Asset. In additional, each time on enemy Psyker unti is destroyed, you can generate one Command Asset. In either case, neither player can generate more than 3 Command Assets each turn as the result of this twist.

3. #### Orbital Debris

At the start of each Action phase, starting with the player who has the Initiative this turn, each player selects one point on the battlefield before rolling on D6 for every unit within 6” of that point. Subtract 1 from the result if the unit being rolled for is a Character; on a 4+, place on blast marker next to the unit being rolled for.

4. #### Acid Rain

At the start of each Action phase, the player who has the Initiative this turn rolls one D12. On 7+ it is raining this turn; until the end of the turn, subtract 1 from hit rolls made for ranged weapons and subtract 1” from the Movement characteristic of all units, to a min of 0”.

5. #### Surprise Attack

After both players have deployed their armies, they must roll off. The winner has launched a surprise attack or counter-attack. For the remainder of the battle, each time the players roll for Initiative, the player who launched the surprise attack or counter-attack rolls one D12, but their opponent only rolls one D6. In addition, each time a player is determined to have the Initiative during this battle, they can generate one Command Asset.

6. #### Slay the Warlords

At the end of each Damage phase, before checking to see if any Command Assets need to be discarded, each player generates one Command Asset for each enemy Warlord that is not a Warmaster \- and D3 Command Assets for an enemy Warlord that is a Warmaster \- that was destroyed in that phase.

7. #### Grudge match

Units automatically pass any Morale tests they have to take during this battle.

8. #### Battle Frenzy

Each time one of your opponent’s Detachments is destroyed, you generate one Command Asset.

9. #### Set Piece Battle

Do not use the Deployment rules on page 74\. Instead, the Defender must first set up their entire army on the battlefield, and then the Attacker must do likewise. Units must be set up wholly within their own deployment zone, unless they have an ability that allows them to be set up as Tactical Reserves. Once both armies have been deployed, players then nominate their Commanders select Warmasters prepare Command Assets and resolve any pre-battle abilities.

10. #### Many Paths to Victory

The Defender rolls one D12 on the Objective table, re-rolling if the result is the same as the objective you have already generated; use both results for the forthcoming battle. If both results require you to set up objective markers on the battlefield, make sure you can tell which objective markers relate to which objective result.

11. #### Double or Nothing

The Defender rolls on D12. On a 1-6, no Twists are used for this battle. On a 7-12, roll twice more on this table (re-roll any duplicate results and any further results of Double or Nothing) and apply both results to the table.

12. #### Strategic Escalation

Before the players being deployment, each player splits their army into two contingents, each containing (as equally as possible) the same number of Detachments. Players can only deploy the Detachments from one of their contingents at the start of the battle \- the rest are placed into Strategic Reserves. Note that Fortification Network Detachments cannot be placed into Strategic Reserves \- these must be placed in the contingent that is deployed at the start of the battle.  

Starting from the second turn, beginning with the Defender, each player can select one Detachment that is currently in Strategic Reserves and set it up on the battlefield during the Set Up Reinforcements step. After these Detachments has been set up, starting with the Defender, the players alternate making a reserves roll for each of their other Detachments that are still in Strategic Reserves by rolling one D12; on a 7+ that Detachment immediately arrives from Strategic Reserves, otherwise it must remain in Strategic Reserves this turn (but it may arrive in later turns). Add 1 to a Detachment arrives from Strategic Reserves, every one of its units must be set up wholly within 12” of a battlefield edge, and more than 1” from any enemy units. 

A Detachment must be issued an Advance order on the turn in which it is set up, but units in that Detachment cannot make their Move action this turn (it is assumed this action was used to move onto the battlefield). Units that have the Deep Strike ability can instead now use that ability to set up on the battlefield. Any unit that is still in Strategic Reserves at the end of the battle counts as having been destroyed.

## Ruse Table {#ruse-table}

The Defender secretly rolls a d6, to select their Ruse. They don’t have to reveal the ruse until they use it.  
If the Attacker’s points are within 3 points of the Defender, the Attacker rolls a D12 on the Ruse table. If they roll a 1-6, they get the appropriate Ruse.

1. #### Ambush

Before deployment, select one Detachment from your army \- this cannot be a Super-heavy, Super-heavy Auxiliary or Fortification Network Detachment. Every unit in that Detachment gains the Deep Strike ability.

2. #### Dug In

During the first turn, in the Damage phase, the Defender rolls D12s instead of D6s when making saving throws for any units in their army that have not moved this turn.

3. #### The Blood of Martyrs

Each time one of the Defender’s Warlords is destroyed during this battle, the Defender generates one Command Asset.

4. #### Inspired Tactician

Once per turn, when the Defender selects one of their Detachments to carry out its order but before any actions are made by units in that Detachment (excluding free actions), the Defender can, if their Warmaster is on the battlefield, change that Detachment's order to Advance, Aimed Fire or Assault.

5. #### Brilliant Strategist

The Defender generates one extra Command Asset during the Generate Command Assets step in any turn in which their Warmaster is on the battlefield.

6. #### Tactical Reserves

Once per battle, in the Set Up Reinforcements step, the Defender can select on of their units that has been destroyed (excluding Characters and units with the Fortifications Battlefield Role) and set it up on the battlefield wholly within 9” of any battlefield edge and more than 9” from any enemy units (if the unit has the Deep Strike ability, it can be set up according to that ability instead). This unit is a new separate Detachment.

## Sudden Death Table {#sudden-death-table}

As an optional way to play, you may play a game where a Defender starts out at ½ the level of points as the Attacker’s army. In such a case, the Defender gains a Sudden Death Victory Condition. If they succeed in this Condition, they win the game.

1. #### Kill Order

This Sudden Death victory condition is met if, at the end of the battle, the most powerful enemy Detachment has been destroyed. The most powerful enemy Detachment is the one that, when all of its unit’ Power Ratings are added together, has the highest total (if two or more at tied, this victory condition is met if any of them are destroyed).

2. #### Blunt

This Sudden Death victory condition is met if, at the end of the battle, at least half of the total number of units in the battle, at least half of the total number of units in the enemy army have been destroyed.

3. #### Endure

This Sudden Death victory condition is met if, at the end of the battle, at least one Warlord from your army is on the field.

4. #### Vital Ground

This Sudden Death victory condition is met if, at the end of the battle, at least one Warlord from your army is on the battlefield.

5. #### Vendetta

This Sudden Death victory condition is met if, at the end of the battle, the enemy Warmaster has been destroyed.

6. #### Hold the Line

This Sudden Death victory condition is met if, at the end of the battle, no enemy units are wholly within your deployment zone (if you have more than one deployment zone, this victory condition is met so long as no enemy unit is wholly within any of your deployment zones).

## War Zone: Fog of War {#war-zone:-fog-of-war}

### Low Visibility

Ranges have low visibility. Attacks 24-48” have \-1 penalty to-hit. Attacks over 48” have \-2 penalty to-hit.

### Contact Lost

Each time a Detachment finishes carrying out its order in the Action phase, flip over its order marker so it is face down instead of removing it. In the Issue Orders step of the Orders phase, before choosing orders, roll a D12 for each detachment. If it is over the commander’s leadership, they must keep the same order instead of choosing a new order.

## War Zone: Firestorm {#war-zone:-firestorm}

### Burning Skies

Each time an Aircraft unit performs a Move action, they must roll a D6. On a 1, place one blast marker next to that unit.

### Meteoric Debris

At the end of the Order Phase, each player chooses a point on the table. Shift those points 2D12 in a random direction.  If the point leaves the table, stop moving the point at the edge of the table. Light units within 6” of those points take a blast marker, and Heavy and Super-Heavy units take 2 blast markers.

## War Zone: Warp Storm {#war-zone:-warp-storm}

#### Psychic Amplification

Weapons with Witchfire gain Destroyer. The range of Psychic power Command Assets is doubled. When you roll the first D12 for the power, on an 8+, you can keep the card instead of discarding it, but can’t use it again this turn. If you roll a 1, consult the Perils table.

#### The Terrors of the Warp

Units that are not Daemon or Grey Knights get \-1 Leadership, and Legiones Daemonica add 1 to their saving throws

#### Perils Table

1-2. **Cataclysmic Overload**. The psyker is destroyed, and place a blast marker within 6” of all units.  
3-4. **Sucked into the Warp**. The psyker using the power is destroyed.  
5-6. **Psychic Backlash.** The Psyker using the power takes a blast marker.

## War Zone: Sector Imperialis {#war-zone:-sector-imperialis}

#### Height Advantage

Choose which terrain is classified as Sector Imperialis.  Reroll hit rolls of 1 for ranged attacks from targets on that terrain.

#### Roads

Choose which terrain are roads. When units move entirely on those roads during a move, add 4” to their movement, unless they have Fly.

## War Zone: Death World Forest {#war-zone:-death-world-forest}

### Deadly Terrain

Agree upon which terrain features are Death World Forest. Roll a D6 each time a unit ends a Move within 3” of that terrain. On a 1, they take a blast marker.

### Unnatural Growth

Each time players tie when rolling for initiative, place one new Death World Forest terrain on the battlefield more than 1” away from any units. If you don’t have more, move 1 Death Forest Terrain 6” in a random direction, stopping if it would move within 1” of a unit.

## War Zone: Sector Mechanicus {#war-zone:-sector-mechanicus}

### Smoke and Smog

Agree upon which terrain features are Sector Mechanicus. Subtract 1 from hit rolls for attacks made with ranged weapons that target units obscured even a bit by Sector Mechanicus terrain features.

### Plasma Conduits

Agree upon which terrain features are Plasma Conduits. Whilst a unit is within 3” of any Plasma Conduit Terrain, ranged weapons gain the Supercharged ability.

## War Zone: Wall of Martyrs {#war-zone:-wall-of-martyrs}

### Preliminary Bombardment

Choose which terrain features are the Wall of Martyrs. Any units not within 3” of the Wall of Martyrs, roll a d6 for the. On a 1, that unit takes a blast marker.

### Big Guns Never Tire

Add 1 to hit rolls for attacks made with Heavy Weapons that target Buildings or are made by buildings.

## War Zone: Defender World {#war-zone:-defender-world}

Add 1 to the leadership of the Faction that world is.  
Orbital Defence \- Units from the Faction gain Deep strike.  
Sacred World \- Re-roll failed Morale tests for the Faction.  
Relics \- If the Faction controls the most objective markers, they gain D3 Command Assets.
